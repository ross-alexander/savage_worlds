# Player Characters

## James Robert Pentagast (Ross)

Youngest son of Albert Pentgast II, a blue blooded member of the
Alabama aristocracy.

After a youth of wine, women and horse racing he loses his early
inheritance when the Confederacy army seizes his shipment of fine
drinks off a train from New York.  His brother then marries his
childhood sweetheart and takes his finest horse as a "wedding
present".

After stealing back his horse he burns his brother's newly built
family home to the ground and flees to Mexico.

Now out for revenge against the Confederacy and the whole Southern
Establishment.

James is a well kept man in his late 20s, despite living rough for the
last few months.  His suit and boots are worn and dusty, despite his
efforts to keep them clean.

## El Gavilán (Tim)

Lean and sun-browned, with amber eyes.  Black hair tied back with a
red cloth, stubble always just past respectability.  Wears a faded
cavalry jacket, a frayed serape, and carries a sabre and revolver.

Esteban "El Gavilán" Torres (formally Cruz) was a decorated cavalry
sergeant who was relieved of his duties when a dispute with his
superiors turned violent.  Now he rides the Rio Grande border as a
smuggler and "escort," ferrying goods, gossip, and guns.

## Clara Hensley (Paul)

Clara Hensley was born in the wooded ridges of southern Missouri. Her
father, Thomas Hensley, was a trapper and hunter who raised his
daughters to survive in a land where law was scarce, and Clara was the
best shot among them.

When the war broke out, the Hensleys tried to stay neutral.  That
ended when a Confederate guerrilla band raided their homestead,
claiming the family was hiding Union partisans.  Her father stood his
ground and was shot dead on his porch. The raiders burned the house
with her mother and younger sisters inside.

Clara survived.  The men who did it did not.

She hunted them down, one by one, over the next week.  When the last
man tried to beg, she told him to get on his knees if he wanted mercy
— and then shot him through the mouth when he did.

For months afterward, she waged her own private war — ambushing
Confederate patrols, shooting scouts from the treeline, burning supply
wagons, and leaving no witnesses who didn't talk first.  The few who
survived her said it wasn't her aim that frightened them most, it was
her eyes.

Before she pulled the trigger, she'd fix them with a stare, calm and
empty.  After a while, the stories spread.  Soldiers whispered of
"Cold-Eye," the woman who stared through men before she sent them to
their graves.

To Confederates, she was a vengeful ghost.  To some Union soldiers,
she was a problem — a civilian killing at will, without orders or
restraint.

By autumn, a Union major sent men to find her.  She expected a firing
squad; instead, she found an offer.

## Jean-Jacques Attali (Max)

Jean-Jaques is a slim dark-complexioned man with a neat moustache and
combed back hair under a black derby hat.  He typically wears a
battered but once-expensive grey coat and waistcoat and old but
well-made boots.  He spends his days tending to poor Mexicans and
Indians, paying his billls through occasional private-client work for
a handful of better off families in Matamoros who want a European
doctor.  He abhors slavery and all who profit from it.

He typically carries his doctor's bag and a concealed Derringer.  He
carries a harmonica inside a pocket and on social evenings can be seen
with his violin in its case.  He owns a Springfield rifle but normally
leaves it at home. His wife also owns a double-barrelled shotgun which
is kept at home for protection.

Jean-Jacques, sometimes called JJ or John-Jack, is French-Jewish by
background though he converted to Catholicism in order to marry his
wife Consuela, who was concerned that any children be raised as
Catholics.  They have a two-year old son, Gabriel.

# Intro

By chance during your intelligence gathering missions (hanging out at
the saloon) on of you fell in with a confederate youth (somewhat worse
for drink) who confided with pride his role as bodyguard for an
important courier travelling on tomorrow's stage.

You know that the stage will likely have a man riding shotgun with the
driver.  In your experience they're invariably Mexicans.  If a
stagecoach arrived with the blinds down in Matamoros that would be
because it had been robbed and the passengers would have stripped down
to their undergarments.  The rest presumably being stolen.

You're currently crouched low on a sandy bluff overlooking the road
halfway between Matamoros & Bagdad, awaiting the stagecoach.
